# CeraVe Gen Z Marketing Challenge - Complete Case Brief

## Executive Summary
L'Oréal's CeraVe brand seeks to capitalize on the viral success of the "CeraVe x Michael Cera" Super Bowl campaign to build sustained engagement with Gen Z consumers, driving product discovery and long-term brand loyalty.

---

## 1. ABOUT CERAVE

### Brand Position
- **#1 Dermatologist Recommended Skincare Brand** in the United States
- Founded with dermatologist expertise
- Known for science-backed, affordable skincare

### Core Product Philosophy
- **3 Essential Ceramides**: Ceramide NP, Ceramide AP, Ceramide EOP
- Helps restore and maintain the skin's natural barrier
- MVE Technology (Multivesicular Emulsion) for controlled release of ingredients

### Brand Values
- Dermatologist-developed
- Accessible/affordable pricing
- Science-first approach
- No unnecessary ingredients

---

## 2. THE MICHAEL CERA CAMPAIGN SUCCESS

### Campaign Overview
- **Super Bowl 2024** campaign featuring actor Michael Cera
- Played on the name similarity: "CeraVe" ↔ "Michael Cera"
- Created conspiracy theory-style teaser content suggesting Michael Cera invented CeraVe

### Campaign Results (Massive Success)
| Metric | Result |
|--------|--------|
| Earned Media Value | **$15.4 Million** |
| Total Impressions | **32 Billion** |
| Social Engagement | Unprecedented viral spread |
| Brand Awareness | Significant uplift among younger consumers |

### Why It Worked
1. **Humor & Self-Awareness**: Didn't take itself too seriously
2. **Cultural Relevance**: Tapped into conspiracy theory/internet culture
3. **Celebrity Authenticity**: Michael Cera's awkward persona was perfect fit
4. **Multi-Platform Strategy**: Teaser content + Super Bowl reveal + follow-up

## 3. THE GEN Z SKINCARE LANDSCAPE

### "#GenSkincare Era" Key Insights

#### Early Adopters
- Gen Z starts skincare routines at **average age 16** (vs. 25 for older generations)
- Skincare is integral to their identity and self-care rituals
- View skincare as "self-care" not just vanity

#### Information Sources
1. **Social Media** (Primary): TikTok, Instagram, YouTube
2. **Dermatologists**: Highly trusted; "derm-approved" = credibility
3. **Peer Recommendations**: Authentic reviews matter
4. **Influencers**: But only authentic ones; can detect paid promotion

#### Gen Z Skincare Behaviors
- **Ingredient-Conscious**: Research specific ingredients (retinol, niacinamide, ceramides)
- **Routine-Oriented**: Multi-step routines are common
- **Value-Driven**: Want quality at accessible prices
- **Sustainability-Aware**: Care about brand ethics and environmental impact
- **Skeptical of Marketing**: Authenticity is non-negotiable

#### Where They Discover Products
- TikTok (#SkinTok)
- Instagram Reels
- YouTube reviews
- Reddit (r/SkincareAddiction)
- Peer recommendations

## 4. THE CHALLENGE

### Primary Objective
> "Build on the momentum of the Michael Cera campaign to engage Gen Z in a way that drives **product discovery** and **brand loyalty**."

### Strategic Goals
1. **Sustain Viral Momentum**: Keep CeraVe culturally relevant post-Super Bowl
2. **Drive Product Discovery**: Get Gen Z to try specific products
3. **Build Long-Term Loyalty**: Convert trial into repeat purchase and advocacy
4. **Maintain Brand Authenticity**: Stay true to dermatologist-backed, science-first positioning

---

## 5. PRODUCT FOCUS (3 Hero Products)

### Product 1: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream
- **Format**: Iconic tub/jar
- **Key Benefits**:
  - 24-hour hydration
  - 3 essential ceramides
  - Fragrance-free
  - For normal to dry skin
- **USP**: The cult-favorite "cream in the tub"

### Product 2: CeraVe Healing Ointment
- **Format**: Tube/jar
- **Key Benefits**:
  - Protects and soothes dry, cracked skin
  - Non-comedogenic
  - Multi-purpose (lips, cuticles, dry patches)
  - Petrolatum-based with ceramides
- **USP**: Versatile healing solution

### Product 3: CeraVe AM Facial Moisturizing Lotion with SPF 30
- **Format**: Pump bottle
- **Key Benefits**:
  - Broad spectrum SPF 30
  - Lightweight, non-greasy
  - Contains niacinamide and hyaluronic acid
  - Daily UV protection + hydration
- **USP**: Simplified morning routine (moisturizer + SPF in one)

## 11. STRATEGIC CONSIDERATIONS

### Opportunities
1. Leverage Michael Cera association (but evolve it)
2. Tap into #SkinTok culture
3. Emphasize dermatologist credibility with Gen Z
4. Create shareable, meme-worthy content
5. Build community around "skin barrier" education

### Challenges
1. Maintaining authenticity post-viral moment
2. Standing out in crowded skincare market
3. Converting awareness to actual purchase
4. Competing with trendier/cooler brands
5. Sustaining engagement beyond one campaign

### Key Questions to Answer
1. How do we keep the Michael Cera momentum without being repetitive?
2. What makes Gen Z actually buy (not just engage)?
3. How do we differentiate the 3 hero products?
4. What platform strategy reaches Gen Z effectively?
5. How do we measure brand loyalty (not just awareness)?
